— Our manifesto —

For a romantic repair of our generation.

The founding text of ALMA. For those who sense that something has broken in how our era makes us meet.

We are the generation that learned to love behind a screen, to choose behind a swipe, to judge behind a filter. And today we discover what that cost us.

The reality

Sixty-three percent of men under 30 are now single. The vast majority of women's messages on dating apps go to the top fifth of men. A fifty-billion-dollar industry thrives each year on the loneliness it manufactures.

These numbers are not an accident. They are the direct result of a business model that has turned romantic encounter into commodity, and that profits when we remain trapped in the game.

Tinder doesn't make money when you find love. Tinder makes money when you keep searching.

What broke

A quiet fracture has formed between the men and women of our generation. Men accumulate a feeling of not existing, convinced they are worth nothing because no one swipes on their photo. Women accumulate an exhaustion that turns into mistrust, worn down by inappropriate messages, dates that go wrong, ghosts who disappear.

These two populations don't even know each other. But a technology has managed to make them resent each other in advance. They are the crossed victims of a system that damages both, in different ways.

Sherry Turkle warned us, more than a decade ago, that we were becoming "alone together." Jonathan Haidt has documented how platforms structurally harm the mental health of young generations. Esther Perel reminds us that intimacy is a skill, not a transaction. They were right. They are still right. We are now living what they predicted.

Meanwhile, the apps that caused this catastrophe continue to claim they are helping.

What we refuse

We refuse the idea that this situation is inevitable. We refuse that technology must inevitably become extractive. We refuse to accept that our loneliness is the fuel of an industry that enriches itself on it.

We refuse the disposable culture applied to love. We refuse the commodification of people. We refuse the war between sexes that these apps amplify and make profitable.

And above all, we refuse the idea that we must choose between destructive technology and an impossible return to a pre-digital world. A third way exists.

What we are building

ALMA is our attempted answer. A dating app that structurally inverts what current apps do destructively. Not a cosmetic improvement: a refoundation.

Three profiles per day, selected for their values. Scheduled thirty-minute conversations. Required to close every exchange with dignity. Forced exit to the real world, in trusted partner venues. Mandatory identity verification. Judicial cooperation for serious offenses.

No advertising, ever. No ranking, ever. No predatory freemium. A clear subscription, three times cheaper than Tinder Premium, that funds what it funds.

Long-term only. All sexual orientations. For people who want to meet someone, not consume profiles.

Our conviction

We believe technology can serve human connection instead of exploiting it. But that requires designing against the grain of the attention economy.

We believe long-term love has become revolutionary in the age of the disposable. To choose to last is now a political act.

We believe the fracture between sexes is not a fate. It is a symptom. And it has a structural cause we can fight.

We believe, finally, that our generation deserves better than what was manufactured for it.

Our commitment

We commit to building ALMA over time. To publishing our real numbers in full transparency. To systematically cooperating with law enforcement for serious offenses. To refusing any compromise with an extractive business model. To listening to those who use us, and to growing with them.

We commit to carrying this discourse publicly, even when it disturbs. Even when we are told it is naive, idealistic, doomed. We know conviction is more contagious than proof, and the best fights always begin by seeming impossible.

Our call

This manifesto is addressed to everyone who senses what we describe. To everyone who left dating apps from exhaustion rather than satisfaction. To everyone who has seen loved ones come out damaged. To everyone who feels we must do things differently.

If you read us and these lines touch you, you are one of us. Join the waitlist. Share this text. Help us build what we want this era to become.

An era where we will have recovered our capacity to love lastingly. An era where technology will serve our intentions instead of diverting them. An era where we will have remembered that what we are looking for is someone.

Someone, not something.

— Hamza El Jawahiry, founder of ALMA
