Who is ALMA?

ALMA is a French dating application currently in development, opening to the Paris public in late 2026. Founded by Hamza El Jawahiry (22), ALMA proposes a refoundation of dating codes: three profiles per day, scheduled 30-minute conversations, mandatory exit to the real world, identity verification, no advertising.

To understand ALMA in depth, read our founding manifesto or discover our complete vision.

Short founder biography

Hamza El Jawahiry, 22, is the founder of ALMA, a French dating application built for long-term relationships. Born in 2004, he has carried the project for three years in response to what he observes around him: a generation damaged by dominant dating apps, and an economic system that thrives on the loneliness it manufactures. ALMA opens in Paris in late 2026.

Frequently asked questions

Why create yet another dating app?

Precisely because dozens already exist, and none offer what ALMA proposes. All share a common business model: thriving on sustained singleness. ALMA is the first built against this principle.

Hamza is 22. Isn't that too young?

At 22, I live exactly what I describe. I'm in the target group, I've observed the problem for five years among the people I love, and I carry this discourse with a legitimacy few older people can claim.

What are the numbers that drive ALMA?

Male singleness among under-30s has exploded over the past decade — a gap of more than 25 points has opened between men and women of the same age. On current apps, the vast majority of female interactions go to a small minority of men, producing frustration and bitterness on both sides. The global dating market exceeds $50 billion in annual revenue and keeps growing, even as user satisfaction declines. If you want precise sources for an article, write to me and I'll send the exact references.

How does ALMA make money?

A single subscription, substantially cheaper than the premium tiers of dominant platforms. Plus a modest commission on Date Packs in our partner restaurants. No advertising, ever. No additional paid features, ever.

How does ALMA protect women?

Mandatory identity verification for everyone, 24/7 human moderation, AI detection of aggression in conversations, geolocated SOS button during physical meetings, systematic judicial cooperation for abuse. A full page details our approach: Safety & Trust.

When will the app be available?

Public launch in Paris late 2026. Waitlist subscribers will be notified first. Expansion to Lyon, Marseille, Bordeaux in 2027, then nationally, then French-speaking Belgium and Switzerland.
